Type: Terrestrial

Size: 5-6 inches

Small Description:
Harpactira pulchripes, also known as the golden blue-legged baboon spider, is a bright yellow bodied and metallic blue legged tarantula found in South Africa.[1] It is a very highly desired tarantula in the European and American tarantula keeping hobby.

Small Description:
Lasiodora parahybana, known in the hobby as the Salmon Pink Birdeater is a New World Terrestrial Tarantula endemic to North Easter Brazil. This species is argued to be the 3rd or 4th largest tarantula in the world growing anywhere from 9-11 inches with most only being about 8-9”.

Small Description:
Heteroscodra maculata lives in tropical warm and humid area. Temperatures almost daily reach levels up to 30°C. The intense sunlight isn’t a big issue due to the treetops., but the spider will protect itself against the heat in tree crevices, hollow branches and leaves.

Ornate Tiger Spider

Poecilotheria ornata

Category: Old World

Type: Arboreal

Location: Sri Lanka

Temperament: Very Defensive

Size: 8-10 inches

Lifespan: 3-12 years

Venom: Medically Significant

Click here for Additional Info

Small Description:
This species is lightning fast with powerful venom. There has never been a human death recorded that came from a tarantula bite, however symptoms include intense pain, muscle cramps, nausea and dizziness. These symptoms have been reported to last weeks and months later in the form of stiff joints, sore muscles, and other issues.
